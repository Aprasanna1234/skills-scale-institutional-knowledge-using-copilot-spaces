# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## UX Designers

### Role Summary
UX Designers focus on understanding user needs, designing intuitive interfaces, and ensuring usability across all customer touchpoints. They collaborate closely with Product Managers and Developers to translate user research into actionable design solutions.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct user research and usability testing
- Create wireframes, prototypes, and design specifications
- Ensure accessibility and inclusive design principles
- Participate in design reviews and provide feedback on implementations
- Validate design decisions with user data and feedback loops

### Goals
- Deliver intuitive, accessible user experiences
- Reduce friction in user workflows
- Ensure design consistency across products and platforms

### Typical Communication
- Design review sessions with developers and product leads
- User research findings and usability test reports
- Design specifications and interaction guidelines
- Feedback on PR implementations related to UI/UX

---

## Data Analysts

### Role Summary
Data Analysts collect, analyze, and interpret data to inform product decisions and measure project outcomes. They enable evidence-based decision-making across Product, Project, and Leadership teams.

### Responsibilities
- Define and track key metrics aligned with project success criteria
- Analyze project outcomes and provide insights on performance
- Create dashboards and reports for stakeholder visibility
- Support A/B testing and experimentation frameworks
- Identify trends, patterns, and opportunities for improvement

### Goals
- Inform data-driven decision-making
- Measure and communicate project impact
- Enable continuous improvement through actionable insights

### Typical Communication
- Weekly metrics dashboards and summary reports
- Data analysis findings and recommendations to Product and Project teams
- Post-launch outcome reports and success metrics reviews
- Participation in planning and retrospective meetings

---

## DevOps Engineers

### Role Summary
DevOps Engineers manage infrastructure, build pipelines, and deployment automation. They ensure system reliability, security, and operational excellence throughout the development and release lifecycle.

### Responsibilities
- Design and maintain CI/CD pipelines and infrastructure
- Automate build, test, and deployment processes
- Monitor system performance, security, and reliability
- Support developers with deployment and operational issues
- Plan and execute safe, repeatable release processes

### Goals
- Ensure fast, reliable, and secure deployments
- Minimize deployment risk and rollback complexity
- Maintain high system availability and performance

### Typical Communication
- CI/CD configuration and automation documentation
- Release readiness reviews and deployment planning
- Incident response and post-mortem participation
- Infrastructure and tooling updates in team syncs

---

## Customer Support Leads

### Role Summary
Customer Support Leads represent the voice of end users and support teams. They surface customer feedback, operational pain points, and support challenges to inform product and delivery decisions.

### Responsibilities
- Gather and synthesize customer feedback and support tickets
- Surface high-impact customer issues and feature requests
- Participate in release planning to anticipate support needs
- Ensure knowledge transfer and documentation for new features
- Provide end-user perspective during design and planning discussions

### Goals
- Reduce support burden through preventive improvements
- Ensure customer success and satisfaction
- Improve product usability based on real-world usage patterns

### Typical Communication
- Customer feedback summaries and trends
- Support readiness reviews before feature releases
- Documentation and training material feedback
- Participation in release planning and post-launch reviews

---

## Cross-functional Interaction Patterns

### Example: Feature Delivery Workflow

1. **Planning Phase**: Product Manager defines success metrics with Data Analyst. UX Designer conducts user research. Project Manager coordinates dependencies.
2. **Design Phase**: UX Designer creates wireframes. Developers provide technical feedback. Customer Support Lead advises on support implications.
3. **Development Phase**: Developers implement. DevOps Engineer ensures CI/CD readiness. Data Analyst prepares tracking instrumentation.
4. **Testing Phase**: QA validates. UX Designer conducts usability testing. DevOps Engineer runs deployment smoke tests.
5. **Release Phase**: Project Manager coordinates timeline. DevOps Engineer manages deployment. Customer Support Lead prepares documentation and support materials.
6. **Post-Release Phase**: Data Analyst measures outcomes. Customer Support Lead monitors support issues. Product Manager gathers feedback for next iteration.
